# Project Domain — Tadnun / Moroccan Digital Transformation

Deep domain knowledge for the Tadnun project. Use this context when making
content decisions, writing copy, or designing features.

## Market Context

**Tadnun** targets Moroccan SMEs that are still largely analog or use fragmented
digital tools. The platform positions itself as a sector-specific digital
transformation partner — not a generic web agency.

### Key Market Facts
- Morocco is rapidly digitizing ahead of the **2030 FIFA World Cup**
- Many SMEs still rely on paper, WhatsApp, and word-of-mouth
- Trust is built through **sector expertise**, not generic promises
- French is the business language; Arabic/Darija is the emotional language
- Price sensitivity is high — ROI must be clearly demonstrated

### Sectors (Current — Extensible)
The current sectors are a subset. New sectors can be added at any time.

| Sector | Key Pain Points |
|--------|----------------|
| Agriculture & Cooperatives | Traceability, export compliance, cooperative payments |
| Restaurants & Cafes | Google visibility, delivery platforms, food waste |

| Retail & Commerce | Online presence, WhatsApp catalog, inventory |
| Education & Training | Parent portal, enrollment, e-learning |
| Real Estate | Lead capture, multi-platform listings, virtual tours |
| Logistics & Transport | Proof of delivery, route optimization, fleet management |

### Moroccan-Specific Integrations
- **CMI** — Morocco's main payment processor
- **ONSSA** — Food safety authority (agriculture)
- **CNSS/AMO** — Social security / health insurance
- **DGSN** — National security (tourism registration)
- **Glovo/Jumia** — Delivery platforms popular in Morocco

### Tone & Voice
- **Authoritative but approachable** — we understand your sector
- **Problem-first** — lead with the pain, then the solution
- **Local credibility** — reference Moroccan cities, regulations, platforms
- **Urgency without pressure** — competitors are digitizing, don't fall behind

### Content Guidelines
- Testimonials reference real Moroccan cities (Casablanca, Marrakech, Agadir, etc.)
- Stats should be specific and believable (not "10x growth")
- FAQs address real concerns (cost, complexity, timeline, Arabic support)
- CTAs should feel like an invitation, not a hard sell

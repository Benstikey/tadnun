# Content Strategy — Tadnun
## 3-Month Content Roadmap (Q2 2026)

*Last updated: 2026-03-27*

---

## Current State

Tadnun's site has **zero educational content** — no blog, no case studies, no guides. The site consists of:
- Homepage with sector overview
- 8 individual sector pages (agriculture, restaurants, tourism, healthcare, retail, education, real estate, logistics)
- About page
- Approach page
- Contact page (WhatsApp-first)

This means Tadnun captures **zero organic search traffic** from SME owners researching their problems. The only visitors are those who already know Tadnun exists or land on a sector page directly.

**Opportunity:** Moroccan SME owners search in French for their problems daily. Nobody is creating high-quality, Morocco-specific digital transformation content in French. The field is wide open.

---

## Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: "Mon secteur a un probleme" (Awareness)
*Buyer stage: Problem-aware, not solution-aware*

The business owner feels pain but hasn't connected it to "digital transformation." They search for symptoms, not solutions.

- Content type: Problem-focused articles, sector guides, cost-of-inaction calculators
- Tone: Empathetic, specific, grounded in Moroccan business reality
- CTA: Soft — "Decouvrez comment d'autres [sector] resolvent ce probleme" (links to sector page)
- Keywords: Problem-based, sector-specific, French language

### Pillar 2: "Comment digitaliser mon activite" (Consideration)
*Buyer stage: Solution-aware, evaluating options*

The business owner knows they need to go digital but doesn't know how, what tools exist, or what it costs. They're comparing approaches (DIY, freelancer, agency, SaaS, Tadnun).

- Content type: How-to guides, comparison articles, checklists, tool evaluations
- Tone: Practical, authoritative, transparent about trade-offs
- CTA: Medium — "Reservez votre diagnostic gratuit" (links to contact page)
- Keywords: "Comment digitaliser," solution-focused, tool comparisons

### Pillar 3: "Ca marche vraiment au Maroc" (Decision)
*Buyer stage: Provider-aware, choosing who to work with*

The business owner is ready to act but needs proof it works, especially in Morocco with Moroccan constraints (infrastructure, language, budget, staff skills).

- Content type: Case studies, ROI breakdowns, behind-the-scenes process articles, comparison vs. alternatives
- Tone: Proof-driven, specific numbers, real stories
- CTA: Hard — "Contactez-nous sur WhatsApp" or "Demandez votre devis gratuit"
- Keywords: Brand + sector, "avis," "temoignage," result-oriented

### Pillar 4: "Le Maroc digital en 2030" (Thought Leadership)
*Buyer stage: All stages — builds trust and domain authority*

Positions Tadnun as the authority on Moroccan digital transformation. Leverages the 2030 World Cup urgency and the 93% of SMEs without digital presence.

- Content type: Trend articles, regulatory explainers, market data, opinion pieces
- Tone: Forward-looking, data-backed, confident without being preachy
- CTA: Varies — newsletter signup, sector page links
- Keywords: "Digitalisation Maroc," "PME Maroc 2030," regulatory terms

## Distribution Strategy

### Channel Mix

| Channel | Audience | Content Type | Frequency | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------------|-----------|-------|
| **LinkedIn** | Decision-makers, B2B, urban SMEs | All pillars, especially P2 + P4 | 3x/week | French only. Short post with key insight + link. Tag sector hashtags (#RestaurateursMaroc, #HotellerieMaroc). Use personal profiles (founder) for higher reach. |
| **Facebook** | Broader SME audience, less tech-savvy | P1 awareness content, case studies | 3x/week | French + occasional Darija in comments. Facebook groups for sector targeting (restaurateurs, hoteliers, commerçants). Visual-first: use OG images with stat callouts. |
| **WhatsApp Groups** | Existing contacts, sector communities | Case studies, checklists, new articles | 1x/week | Share article link with 2-sentence teaser. Do not spam. Join existing sector groups (cooperative networks, hotel associations, restaurant owner groups). |
| **WhatsApp Status** | Personal network of founders | Behind-the-scenes, results, tips | Daily | Short-form, authentic content. "On a lance la fiche Google de Karim hier. Ce matin il a deja 3 appels." |
| **Google organic** | Search-driven visitors | All blog posts | Ongoing (SEO) | Primary long-term channel. Optimize for featured snippets on how-to queries. Target Position 0 for FAQ-style content. |

### Content Repurposing Flow

Each blog post produces:
1. **Full article** (blog) — primary asset
2. **LinkedIn post** — key insight in 150 words + link
3. **Facebook post** — visual card with stat + 2-sentence hook + link
4. **WhatsApp teaser** — 2 lines + link for group sharing
5. **Instagram carousel** (Month 3+) — 5-slide summary for visual learners

### Internal Linking Architecture

```
Blog posts (awareness)
  ↓ link to
Sector pages (consideration)
  ↓ link to
Contact page (conversion)

Blog posts (consideration)
  ↓ link to
Approach page + Sector pages
  ↓ link to
Contact page

Blog posts (decision / case studies)
  ↓ link to
Contact page (direct CTA)
  + backlink to relevant sector page
```

**Specific linking rules:**
- Every blog post MUST link to at least 1 sector page
- Every blog post MUST have a CTA band at the bottom linking to /contact
- Case studies link to 2 pages: the sector page + the contact page
- Comparison articles link to /approach (how we work) + /about (why us)
- Cross-link between related blog posts (e.g., "Commission Booking.com" ← → "Riad case study")
- Sector pages should link back to their relevant blog posts in a "Ressources pour votre secteur" section

## Content Production Notes

### Language Priority
1. **French first** — every piece is written and published in French
2. **Arabic second** — translate within 1 week of French publication. Prioritize awareness articles (P1) for Arabic translation first, as these reach the broadest audience.
3. **English third** — translate only high-performing articles (>500 views in first month). English versions serve MRE audiences and diaspora investors.

### Source Material
Each piece should draw from existing data in the codebase:
- `src/data/sector-details.ts` — pain points, testimonials, ROI stats, workflows, FAQ, integrations (rich source material for every sector)
- `src/messages/fr.json` — sector descriptions, pain summaries, solution summaries, pricing, FAQ answers
- `.agents/product-marketing-context.md` — personas, objections, customer quotes, competitive positioning, proof points

### Writing Guidelines
- **Voice:** Warm, direct, specific. Problem-first. Use real MAD figures, real scenarios, real cities.
- **Words to use:** digitalisation, blocages, votre secteur, concu pour le Maroc, darija, simple, sans engagement, WhatsApp, problemes concrets
- **Words to avoid:** IA, disruptif, cutting-edge, innovant, plateforme, enterprise, revolution digitale, synergie, leverage, scalable
- **Structure:** Every article opens with a scenario (not a definition). Lead with the pain, not the solution. Use the customer's own words from testimonials.
- **CTAs:** Soft in awareness content ("Decouvrez les solutions pour votre secteur"). Hard in decision content ("Contactez-nous sur WhatsApp — reponse en 23 min").
- **Length:** 1,200-2,500 words. Long enough to rank, short enough to respect busy owners.

### SEO Notes
- Target 1 primary keyword + 2-3 secondary keywords per article
- Include FAQ section at bottom (helps featured snippets + FAQ schema)
- Use French-language keywords — the Moroccan search market is overwhelmingly French
- Meta descriptions should include a number or statistic (e.g., "40% de clients en plus")
- URL slugs in French, kebab-case (e.g., `/blog/comment-apparaitre-google-maps-restaurateur-maroc`)

### Content Calendar Rhythm
- **Publication:** 2 articles per week (Tuesday + Thursday)
- **Process:** Brief (Monday) → Draft (Tuesday-Wednesday) → Review (Thursday) → Publish (Friday for next week)
- **Arabic translation:** 1 week delay from French publication
- **Social distribution:** Same day as publication (LinkedIn + Facebook), next day (WhatsApp groups)

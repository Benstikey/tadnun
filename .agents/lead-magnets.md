# Lead Magnets Strategy — Tadnun

*Last updated: 2026-03-27*

---

## Context

Tadnun currently has **one conversion path**: contact us (WhatsApp or form). There are no downloadable resources, no gated content, no email list beyond post-contact nurture. This means every visitor either converts immediately or leaves with no way to re-engage them.

**The problem:** Moroccan SME owners are tech-hesitant. They rarely contact a service provider on the first visit. They need time, proof, and familiarity before reaching out. Without an intermediate conversion step, Tadnun loses 95%+ of visitors permanently.

**The solution:** Lead magnets that capture email addresses in exchange for genuinely useful, sector-specific content. These feed into the nurture email sequence (see `email-sequence.md`) and keep Tadnun top-of-mind until the prospect is ready to act.

**Audience reality:**
- French-speaking Moroccan business owners
- Many are not used to downloading PDFs or reading long guides
- WhatsApp is the dominant communication channel
- Practical, concrete, and short content performs best
- Numbers (MAD figures, percentages, timelines) build credibility
- Visual content (infographics, checklists) is more accessible than long text

## 3. Distribution Strategy

### Embedding CTAs in Existing Sector Pages

Each of the 8 sector pages already has pain points, ROI stats, testimonials, and workflows. Lead magnet CTAs should be woven into these existing sections, not bolted on awkwardly.

**Placement strategy per sector page:**

1. **After Pain Points Section** (inline CTA)
   - "Ces problemes vous parlent ? Telechargez notre [checklist/guide] gratuit."
   - This is the moment of maximum resonance — they just read about their problems.

2. **After ROI Section** (inline CTA)
   - "Decouvrez comment ces resultats sont possibles pour votre [sector]."
   - Leverages the "I want that too" moment.

3. **Sticky Bottom Bar** (persistent, non-intrusive)
   - Small bar at the bottom of the screen: "Guide gratuit : [sector-specific title] — Telechargez"
   - Appears after 30 seconds on page or 50% scroll depth.

| Agriculture | Checklist (universal) + Case study (Fatima) | "Evaluez votre cooperative en 2 minutes" |

| Tourism | Booking.com commission guide (#5) | "Reduisez vos commissions Booking de 50%" |
| Healthcare | Checklist (universal) | "Votre cabinet perd-il de l'argent sans le savoir ?" |

### Exit-Intent Popup Strategy

**Which pages get exit-intent popups:**

| Page | Popup Content | Rationale |
|------|--------------|-----------|
| Homepage | Checklist (universal) | Broadest audience, checklist fits everyone |
| Sector pages (each) | Sector-specific lead magnet if available, else checklist | Highest intent visitors — they're researching their sector |
| Approach page | Infographic (cost vs value) | Visitors here are evaluating "is this for me?" |
| About page | Infographic (cost vs value) | Building trust — proof points reinforce credibility |
| Blog posts (future) | Content upgrade matching the post topic | Contextual relevance drives highest capture rates |

**Popup design principles:**
- Trigger: exit-intent on desktop, 60-second timer on mobile (exit-intent doesn't work well on mobile)
- Frequency cap: show once per session, once per 7 days per visitor
- Design: clean, matches Tadnun's warm editorial aesthetic
- Copy: benefit-first, short, with social proof number ("150+ entreprises servies")
- Form: just name + email (sector is inferred from the page they're on)
- Mobile: slide-up bottom sheet, not full-screen overlay (less intrusive)

## 4. Email Capture Flow

### Form Design

**Minimal fields (3 only):**

| Field | Type | Required | Rationale |
|-------|------|----------|-----------|
| Prenom | Text input | Yes | Personalize emails, build human connection |
| Email | Email input | Yes | Delivery mechanism + nurture sequence entry |
| Secteur | Dropdown (8 options + "Autre") | Yes | Determines which sector-specific nurture track they enter |

**Dropdown options for Secteur:**
- Agriculture
- Restauration
- Tourisme / Hotellerie
- Sante
- Commerce / Retail
- Education / Formation
- Immobilier
- Logistique / Transport
- Autre

**Form UX notes:**
- No phone number field (reduces friction; WhatsApp is offered as secondary CTA in the lead magnet itself)
- No company name field (not needed at this stage)
- Submit button text: "Recevoir mon [guide/checklist/infographie] gratuit" (specific to the lead magnet)
- Below button: "Pas de spam. Un email de suivi par semaine maximum."
- RTL layout for Arabic version (when translated)

### Delivery Mechanism

**Instant download + email confirmation (hybrid approach):**

1. **Immediate** (within 2 seconds of form submission):
   - Show a "Merci" confirmation screen with a direct download button
   - The PDF opens in a new tab / downloads automatically
   - This satisfies instant gratification — critical for a tech-hesitant audience

2. **Email confirmation** (within 60 seconds):
   - Send an email with the PDF attached AND a download link
   - Subject: "{Prenom}, voici votre [guide/checklist] gratuit"
   - This ensures they have it in their inbox for later reference
   - Also trains them to open emails from Tadnun (improves future nurture email deliverability)

3. **WhatsApp follow-up** (optional, Day 1):
   - If the lead magnet was sector-specific, send a WhatsApp message:
     "Bonjour {Prenom}, vous avez telecharge notre guide sur [sector]. Est-ce que ca vous a ete utile ?"
   - This bridges the gap from email to WhatsApp (where real conversations happen in Morocco)

### Post-Download Nurture Integration

**Entry into the email sequence (from `email-sequence.md`):**

The lead magnet download triggers a modified version of the existing 7-email nurture sequence:

| Email | Timing | Adaptation for Lead Magnet Leads |
|-------|--------|----------------------------------|
| Email 1 | Day 0 (immediate) | Already sent as the PDF delivery email — replaces the form confirmation email |
| Email 2 | Day 2 (not Day 1) | Sector-specific pain point mirror — same as existing, but delayed by 1 day since they just received content |
| Email 3 | Day 4 | Social proof / testimonial — same as existing |
| Email 4 | Day 7 | Before/After workflow — same as existing |
| Email 5 | Day 10 | Objection killer — same as existing |
| Email 6 | Day 13 | Urgency (2030 + competitors) — same as existing |
| Email 7 | Day 16 | Final nudge — same as existing, slightly extended timeline since these leads entered higher in the funnel |

**Key difference from contact form leads:**
- Contact form leads are already at bottom-of-funnel — the nurture sequence is 14 days
- Lead magnet leads are earlier in the funnel — the sequence is stretched to 16 days with softer CTAs in the first 2 emails
- Lead magnet leads receive an additional "Did you read the guide? Here's the key takeaway" email on Day 2 before entering the standard pain-point mirror

**Segmentation tags applied at capture:**
- `source: lead-magnet`
- `lead_magnet: [specific magnet name]`
- `sector: [selected sector]`
- `funnel_stage: [tofu/mofu/bofu based on which magnet]`

These tags allow:
- Sector-specific email branching (already built into the nurture sequence)
- Skip logic (if they downloaded a BOFU case study, skip the awareness emails)
- Retargeting (show different lead magnets to those who already downloaded one)

## 7. Dependencies & Prerequisites

Before launching lead magnets, the following must be in place:

1. **Email service provider** — Resend, Loops, or ConvertKit configured with Tadnun domain
2. **Landing page template** — reusable Next.js component for lead magnet download pages
3. **Popup component** — exit-intent + timed popup React component (or third-party like Sumo, OptinMonster)
4. **PDF hosting** — either in `/public/` or on a CDN (Vercel handles this)
5. **Form handler** — server action or API route to capture email, store in DB/email service, trigger delivery
6. **Analytics** — GA4 events for: `lead_magnet_viewed`, `lead_magnet_downloaded`, `lead_magnet_sector`
7. **Blog infrastructure** — for content upgrade distribution (Phase 3 of content strategy)

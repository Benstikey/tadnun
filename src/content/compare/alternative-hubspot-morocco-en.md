## Why look for a HubSpot alternative in Morocco?

If you are reading this page, it is probably because you explored HubSpot -- maybe tested the free CRM, maybe requested a quote for paid plans -- and something does not fit your Moroccan reality. The pricing in dollars, the absence of a Darija interface, the integrations that do not match your local needs, or simply the feeling that this tool was not designed for an SME like yours.

You are not alone. HubSpot is one of the best CRMs in the world for businesses operating in an American or European context. But for Moroccan SMEs with fewer than 20 employees, three problems come up systematically.

## What HubSpot does well

Before going further, let us acknowledge HubSpot's strengths. It is a world-class CRM and marketing automation platform. The free CRM is generous for getting started. The marketing tools -- email sequences, landing pages, lead scoring, reporting -- are among the most complete on the market. The educational content (HubSpot Academy) is excellent. The international integration ecosystem is vast.

If your business relies on digital lead generation, email nurturing and online sales across English-speaking or French-speaking markets, HubSpot is a solid choice.

## Where HubSpot does not fit the Moroccan market

### Dollar pricing unsuited to Moroccan SMEs

HubSpot's free CRM is attractive, but it is deliberately limited. No custom reports, no automation, no email sequences, no removal of HubSpot branding. To access features needed for professional use, you must upgrade to paid plans.

The Starter plan costs 20 USD per user per month. For 10 people: 200 USD per month, roughly 2,000 MAD. The Professional plan -- needed for serious automation, lead scoring and advanced reporting -- costs 100 USD per user per month. For the same 10-person team: 1,000 USD per month, roughly 10,000 MAD. That is 120,000 MAD per year, in dollars, without a single Moroccan integration.

For a Moroccan SME earning in dirhams, this dollar pricing creates a double penalty: a high cost in absolute terms, and uncertainty from exchange rate fluctuations.

### No integration with Moroccan systems

CMI for card payments? Not available. CNSS for social declarations? No. ONSSA for agri-food traceability? HubSpot does not have the concept in its catalog. DGI for tax compliance? No. DGSN for hotel police forms? Same.

HubSpot integrates with Stripe, PayPal, Shopify, Salesforce, Slack -- tools from the American and European markets. But for a Moroccan SME whose daily life runs through CMI, CNSS declarations and payments in dirhams, these integrations are beside the point.

### No Darija, no adoption

HubSpot is available in English, French and standard Arabic (MSA). But 95% of Moroccans communicate in Darija daily. For a kitchen team, agricultural workers or riad receptionists, standard Arabic is a distant language register.

Offering a tool in French or MSA to a team that operates in Darija creates an adoption barrier. The tool will be installed but never used -- or used by one person who becomes a bottleneck.

### A marketing tool, not an operations tool

HubSpot is a marketing and sales CRM. It excels at generating leads, sending automated emails, scoring contacts and producing marketing reports. But most Moroccan SMEs do not have a digital marketing problem -- they have an operational problem.

The restaurateur loses orders. The riad sends 300,000 MAD per year to Booking.com. The cooperative risks an export rejection. The clinic loses 2,000 MAD per day in missed appointments. The retailer discovers a stock-out 10 days after it happens.

HubSpot solves none of these problems. These SMEs do not need marketing automation -- they need operational tools connected to their daily reality.

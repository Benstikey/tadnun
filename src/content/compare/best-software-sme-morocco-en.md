## What Moroccan SMEs actually need

Before comparing options, let's clarify the criteria that matter for a Moroccan SME. They are not the same as for a startup in Paris or a company in Dubai.

93% of Moroccan SMEs have no digital tools. They operate with notebooks, WhatsApp groups, and intuition. For these businesses, the "best software" is not the one with the most features -- it is the one their team will actually use.

Four criteria separate viable solutions from unsuitable ones in the Moroccan market:

1. **Ease of adoption** -- Your team communicates in Darija. They have never used management software. The tool must be as intuitive as a WhatsApp message.
2. **Moroccan integrations** -- CMI for card payments, ONSSA for agri-food exports, CNSS for social security declarations, DGI for taxation. Without these integrations, the software remains disconnected from your reality.
3. **Pricing in dirhams** -- Prices in USD or EUR create budget uncertainty. Moroccan SMEs need clear pricing in MAD, with no surprises.
4. **Human, local support** -- Online documentation is not enough. Moroccan SMEs need on-site training, in their language, with someone who understands their trade.

## The options available in 2026

### Odoo

Odoo is an open-source ERP with over 40,000 modules covering accounting, inventory, HR, CRM, and manufacturing. It is the most cited name when discussing management software in Morocco, thanks to an active network of local integrators.

**Strengths:** Complete functional coverage, large community, free Community edition, Moroccan integrators available.

**Limitations for Moroccan SMEs:** The Enterprise edition costs around 31 EUR per user per month (over 40,000 MAD per year for 10 users). Integration requires a local partner (30,000 to 150,000 MAD in deployment fees). No Darija interface. No native ONSSA, CMI, or WhatsApp Business integrations. Designed for companies with 50+ employees and a technical team.

**Best for:** Companies with 50+ employees, an IT team, and budget for an integrator.

### HubSpot

HubSpot is an American CRM and marketing automation platform. Its free CRM is popular worldwide, and its marketing, sales, and customer service tools are among the most comprehensive on the market.

**Strengths:** Free CRM to start, polished interface, excellent educational content, powerful marketing automation tools, large ecosystem of international integrations.

**Limitations for Moroccan SMEs:** Pricing in USD -- the Starter plan at 20 USD per user per month comes to 2,400+ MAD per month for 10 people. No Darija interface. No CMI, ONSSA, CNSS, or DGI integration. Designed for digital marketing and online sales -- not for the daily operations of a restaurant, cooperative, or clinic. Online-only support, in English or French.

**Best for:** Companies focused on digital marketing with a budget in foreign currency and an English-speaking team.

### Zoho

Zoho is an Indian suite of more than 45 applications covering CRM, accounting, project management, and HR. Less expensive than HubSpot or Salesforce, Zoho is often positioned as the affordable alternative for SMEs.

**Strengths:** More accessible pricing than HubSpot or Salesforce, wide range of interconnected applications, decent CRM for small teams, some features available in French.

**Limitations for Moroccan SMEs:** Pricing in USD (Standard CRM plan: 14 USD per user per month). No Darija. No CMI, ONSSA, or CNSS integration. Configuration requires significant technical work -- Zoho is a self-service platform with dense documentation. No on-site support. No sector specialization for the Moroccan market.

**Best for:** Technically self-sufficient SMEs looking for an affordable CRM with billing in foreign currency.

### Moroccan web agencies

Local web agencies (Casablanca, Rabat, Marrakech) build websites, e-commerce stores, and brand identities. They are numerous, accessible, and communicate in French and Darija.

**Strengths:** Local communication in French and Darija, strong design and branding skills, project flexibility, pricing in MAD.

**Limitations for Moroccan SMEs:** They build websites, not operational tools. A website without CRM, booking management, inventory tracking, or payment integration is a digital brochure -- it does not solve the business's daily problems. No CMI, ONSSA, CNSS, or WhatsApp Business integration. No sector specialization. Support often disappears after delivery.

**Best for:** Companies that only need a showcase website or a simple online store.

### Moroccan freelancers

Freelance developers offer attractive rates (starting from 5,000 MAD for a basic website) and direct communication. They can be found on social media, Facebook groups, and word of mouth.

**Strengths:** Lowest entry price on the market, direct communication, total flexibility, custom projects possible.

**Limitations for Moroccan SMEs:** High risk of dependency on a single person -- if the freelancer is busy, sick, or disappears, your project stops. No team behind them. Rarely sector expertise (they build what you describe, not what you need). No guaranteed ongoing support. No complex Moroccan integrations (CMI, ONSSA, CNSS) unless rare expertise.

**Best for:** One-off projects on a very tight budget, with no need for complex integrations.

## How to choose: the right questions to ask

Before making a decision, ask yourself these questions:

1. **How many people will use the tool daily?** If fewer than 20, an ERP is probably overkill.
2. **Is your team comfortable with technology?** If the answer is no, interface simplicity is the number one criterion.
3. **Do you need Moroccan integrations?** CMI, ONSSA, CNSS, DGI, WhatsApp Business -- if yes, eliminate solutions that do not offer them.
4. **Does your pricing need to be in MAD?** If your cash flow is in dirhams, a subscription in USD or EUR adds exchange rate risk.
5. **Who will train your team?** If no one internally can do it, on-site training is essential.
6. **What happens after deployment?** Check whether ongoing support is included or billed as an extra.
7. **Do you need a generic or sector-specific tool?** A restaurant does not have the same needs as an agricultural cooperative. Sector-specific solutions work from day one.

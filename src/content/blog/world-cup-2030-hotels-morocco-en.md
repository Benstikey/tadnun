## 2030 Is Tomorrow

The World Cup is coming to Morocco. Millions of visitors from around the world will be looking for accommodation -- and they'll be looking online. Not by walking through the medina alleys. Not by asking a friend. On Google, on Booking, on Airbnb.

If your hotel or riad isn't ready, you'll be invisible. Not invisible in a figurative sense -- invisible in the literal sense. The Brazilian tourist won't find you. The Spanish supporter won't know you exist. The Japanese traveler will book with your competitor who has a multilingual site and an international payment system.

The time to prepare is now. Not in 2029.

## What International Visitors Expect

A tourist coming for the World Cup doesn't have the same expectations as a regular traveler. They're coming for a specific event, with fixed dates, in a country they may not know. Here's what they're looking for:

- **A booking site in their language.** Not just French and Arabic. English, Spanish, Portuguese, German -- at minimum.
- **Secure online payment.** With their Visa or Mastercard, not a Moroccan bank transfer.
- **Clear information.** Distance to the stadium, transportation, check-in times.
- **Pre-arrival communication.** Confirmation, directions, WiFi code, local recommendations.
- **Quick check-in.** After 12 hours of travel, nobody wants to fill out a paper form at the front desk.

If you don't offer all of this, someone else will.

## The OTA Commission Problem

Today, the majority of bookings for Moroccan hotels and riads come through Booking.com and Airbnb. And these platforms take 15 to 20% commission on every night.

For a riad at 800 DH per night with 65% occupancy, that's **280,000 to 330,000 DH per year** sent to Booking.com. That's the salary of 2 employees.

During the World Cup, with explosive demand, these commissions will explode too. Every booking that goes through Booking instead of your direct site is money sent to Amsterdam instead of staying in Morocco.

Youssef, riad owner in Fes, did the math: *"In 6 months, we went from 15% to 45% direct bookings. We saved nearly 200,000 DH in commissions this year."*

## The Channel Manager: No More Double Bookings

You manage Booking, Airbnb, Expedia, and your direct site. Four calendars to update manually. A customer books on Booking at 10:15 AM, but you haven't closed the same slot on Airbnb yet. Result: double booking, cancellation, bad review, lost customer.

The channel manager solves this problem. One centralized calendar that syncs all your platforms in real time. A booking comes in on Booking? The slot closes automatically everywhere else. Hotels supported by Tadnun report **zero double bookings** since installing the channel manager.

For a 5-room riad, a single double booking can ruin your week. The channel manager protects every night.

With the digital check-in set up by Tadnun, the guest fills out the form online before arriving. On arrival, check-in takes 2 minutes. Information is pre-filled, and the DGSN form is generated automatically.

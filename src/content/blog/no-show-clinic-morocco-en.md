## The Problem Nobody Puts a Number On

You already know it: every day, 4 to 5 patients don't show up for their appointment. What you may not have calculated is the real cost. At 400 DH per consultation, that's **2,000 DH per day**. Over a month, that's **40,000 DH** in vanished revenue.

And that's not all. Every empty slot is a patient on your waiting list who could have been seen. It's a doctor sitting idle. It's a receptionist who spent time organizing a schedule that falls apart.

Most Moroccan clinics accept no-shows as inevitable. They're not.

## Why Patients Don't Show Up

Before looking for the solution, you need to understand the problem. The main reasons for missed appointments in Morocco:

- **Pure forgetfulness.** The patient booked 3 weeks ago. They forgot. Nobody reminded them.
- **Scheduling conflict.** Something came up, but the patient has no simple way to cancel or reschedule.
- **Friction in booking.** The patient has to call during office hours, gets a busy line, calls back. They end up not coming.
- **No consequences.** The patient knows there's no penalty. They don't bother to notify you.

The good news: three of these four reasons can be solved with simple tools.

The system is simple and proven. Here's how Tadnun sets it up for Moroccan clinics:

An automated SMS or WhatsApp message is sent to the patient 48 hours before the appointment. The message includes the date, time, doctor's name, and address. The patient can confirm, reschedule, or cancel with one click.

### 3-Hour Reminder

A second reminder is sent 3 hours before. It's the last safety net. Clinics supported by Tadnun achieve a **98% reminder delivery rate** -- compared to 50% when the receptionist calls manually.

The patient who can't make it has a simple option: reschedule via a link in the message. The freed-up slot is immediately opened to other patients. No more empty slots, no more time wasted on the phone.

## Online Booking: No More Calls Ringing Into the Void

Another major blocker in Moroccan clinics: booking appointments by phone. The receptionist handles check-ins, files, payments -- and the phone rings. She can't answer. The patient calls back, gets voicemail, gives up.

With online booking, the patient books on their own, 24/7, from their phone. They see available slots, pick one that suits them, and receive instant confirmation via WhatsApp.

Clinics using this system through Tadnun see a 40% drop in incoming calls and better appointment distribution throughout the day.

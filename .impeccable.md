## Design Context

### Users
Moroccan business owners and decision-makers (SMEs) exploring digital transformation for their sector. They arrive via search or referral, browse sector-specific pain points and solutions, and convert via contact form or WhatsApp. Context: professional, often time-constrained, may browse in French, English, or Arabic.

### Brand Personality
**Editorial, warm, clean** — a trusted advisor, not a flashy agency. Confidence meets approachability.

### Emotional Goals
- **Confidence & trust**: "These people know what they're doing"
- **Warmth & approachability**: "I feel comfortable reaching out"
- **Transformation energy**: A subtle sense of progress and possibility

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Subtle sophistication — refined micro-interactions, smooth transitions, polished details
- **Theme**: Light mode only (for now)
- **Palette**: Warm cream (#faf9f7) base, terra-cotta accent (#d4453b), muted neutrals
- **Typography**: Serif italics for personality (Playfair/Fraunces), clean sans-serif for body (Geist Sans), Noto Sans Arabic for RTL
- **Texture**: Subtle grain overlay, editorial whitespace
- **Anti-patterns**: No flashy animations, no gamification, no dark-mode neon aesthetics

### Design Principles
1. **Quiet confidence** — Let quality speak through craft, not volume. Every detail should feel intentional.
2. **Warmth through refinement** — Soft transitions, gentle hover states, and polished micro-interactions create warmth without being playful.
3. **Respect the user's time** — Delight should be noticed subconsciously, never delay or distract from the task.
4. **Cultural sensitivity** — Design must feel equally considered in French, English, and Arabic (RTL). No element should feel like an afterthought in any language.
5. **Progressive trust** — Each interaction should subtly reinforce competence — smooth animations, responsive feedback, and polished states build confidence.
